# U.S. High-Power Rocketry Vendor Directory

This directory is limited to public-facing U.S. hobby vendors and broad category descriptions only. It does **not** include private sellers, informal sources, restricted-material shopping guidance, motor-making instructions, or any workaround for certification, hazmat, age, membership, or transport rules. That compliance-first framing matters because lawful U.S. HPR purchasing and flying are tied to certification and organization rules: the [National Association of Rocketry](https://www.nar.org) and the [Tripoli Rocketry Association](https://www.tripoli.org) both maintain formal high-power certification systems; Tripoli has publicly reminded vendors that they are responsible for verifying member certification levels; and [AeroTech](https://aerotech-rocketry.com) explicitly states age, proof-of-age, and NAR/Tripoli certification requirements for high-power reload purchases.

I included both **motor dealers/manufacturer-direct sellers** and **HPR-support suppliers** such as kit, recovery, electronics, launcher, and retention vendors, because Tripoli’s public vendor/resource system is organized across those same mixed categories and many certified flyers buy from a combination of motor, recovery, electronics, and field-support stores rather than from a single seller.

## Completeness assessment

The **best-covered regions** in this pass were the **Midwest and Upper Midwest** (Illinois, Wisconsin, Missouri), the **Mountain/Intermountain West** (Utah, Colorado), and parts of the **West Coast** (California, Oregon, Washington). Those regions had the clearest combination of current storefronts, Tripoli directory listings, and active product/category pages. The **hardest areas to verify** were vendors whose public presence appears to be mostly **launch-field sales, older dealer pages, or hybrid-only references** rather than a modern e-commerce catalog. That is why several California- and Midwest-linked names ended up in the uncertain/historical section rather than the active table.

The **most useful sources** were, in order, the public [Tripoli Rocketry Association vendor/resource directory](https://www.tripoli.org/vendors,) the official manufacturer pages for [AeroTech](https://aerotech-rocketry.com) and [Loki Research](https://lokiresearch.com,) and public launch-related pages from UROC/LDRS that still listed onsite vendors. The **least useful** source class was old forum chatter: it was sometimes valuable as a discovery lead, but I did not treat forum-only references as sufficient for “active” status unless I could corroborate them with a vendor-owned page, a manufacturer page, a current directory listing, or a recent public event/vendor page.

The most important **gaps** were these. First, I did **not** recover a clean, extractable public U.S. dealer list for [Cesaroni Technology](https://cesaroni.net) in the same way that I did for [AeroTech](https://aerotech-rocketry.com) and [Loki Research](https://lokiresearch.com,) so CTI mapping relied mostly on current retailer pages rather than on a canonical manufacturer dealer-locator page. Second, several long-running hobby vendors had public directory entries but weak recent activity signals, partial site rendering, or unclear state/address information in the pages I could fetch. Third, the hybrid/research-adjacent lane had the most mixed evidence: some names were clearly current, while others looked like surviving informational pages around inactive or partly relaunched brands.

The search terms that produced the highest-value results were close to the ones you suggested: variants on **“high power rocket motors dealer,” “AeroTech dealer,” “CTI / Cesaroni dealer,” “Tripoli launch vendor,” “research motor supplies,” “hybrid rocket motor vendor,”** and state/regional searches. In practice, the recursive expansion that mattered most was **manufacturer page → Tripoli directory → vendor-owned storefront → launch/event/vendor page**. That chain was far more productive than broad web searching alone.

My **confidence level** is **moderate to high** for the vendors in the active table that have a live storefront plus either an official directory entry, an official dealer page, or a recent dated activity marker. Confidence is **moderate** for sellers whose sites are clearly live but whose public pages did not surface a full address, explicit compliance language, or recent dated updates in the fetched snippets. Confidence is **lower** for launch-field vendors and older brand names that now appear mostly through legacy listings, reference pages, or partially active sites.

### Open questions and limitations

A few names almost certainly deserve a second-pass verification if you plan to publish this as a permanent directory: **Bay Area Rocketry, Off We Go Rocketry, Gorilla Rocket Motors, standalone Public Missiles, R.A.T.T. Works, Sky Ripper Systems, and HyperTEK**. In each case, I found enough public evidence to keep them on the radar, but not enough high-confidence current storefront evidence to lift them into the “active” table without qualification. The biggest unresolved structural gap is a clean public dealer list for CTI/Cesaroni comparable to the official dealer pages I was able to find for AeroTech and Loki.
